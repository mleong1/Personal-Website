About Me

I originally started programming in college after just switching from an English degree. A friend of mine showed me some work he was doing for his intro computer science course. He was manipulating the data of a JPEG image to remove a ring from the subject's hand. Coupled with my love for video games, I've heard this can create dangerous expectations for anyone just starting to program. Eventually you come to find that the computer science field isn't coding the next triple-A release, hacking e-mails, seeming intelligent in front of friends, or even sometimes fun. At least right now. Sometimes programming is a frustrated search for a bug while you're pulling your hair out after hours in a lab. But I believe this is a process to get to where you want to go, and though it may be arduous, personally, there is nothing like creating something you're proud of and showing it to peers, friends, or family and getting a positive reaction. That is what has kept me interested in programming. I hope to get that positive reaction in the same, at the time, dangerous way I got into programming, making games. I am interested more generally in how technology shapes social interaction and sometimes can lead to new avenues of interaction. In my free time I program, I run, I try and keep up with the gaming industry, and I play guitar (very much a work in progress, but what isn't).
